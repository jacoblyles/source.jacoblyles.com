About

I grew up in North Carolina in a magical land of pork BBQ, humidity, and college basketball. My family still lives there

I studied economics and mathematics in undergrad at Wake Forest University and computer science in grad school at UCSD

Professional

I'm a product manager and software engineer, working mostly in Python and JavaScript(server/client). But I love working with new technologies, too. I have experience in machine learning, embedded programming, web applications, and mobile development. Most recently I founded Game Closure with 3 friends. Together we developed a kickass SDK, raised over $12 million, and built an awesome team of 30 people. With the company well-established, I recently left to pursue other challenges

In a previous life I was an Investment Banker. The siren call of technology drew me to Silicon Valley. It's impossible for a kid raised on Star Trek to stay away from building the future

Fun

My favorite mathematical proof is Cantor's proof by diagonalization of the uncountability of the real numbers. Greg Egan is my favorite sci fi author. Lately I've been playing with data visualization. Check out my experiments.

I love economics. I keep up with a bunch of econ bloggers and authors while writing a bit myself. Nassim Taleb, Tyler Cowen, Mancur Olson, David Friedman, and Friedrich Hayek are some influences. The relatively new field of Behavioral Economics is also fascinating, and practical!

Other interests include epistemology, poetry, Stoicism, physics, history, classics, music (classical, electronic, and rock), eudaimonia, metaphysics, Muay Thai, MMA, Boxing, rock climbing, scotch whisky, craft beer, and Starcraft (zerg)

Causes

Freedom is undervalued in the intellectual marketplace. Freedom to create, tinker, and explore is a fundamental element of the substrate of progress. I am bullish on the potential for human ingenuity to solve world problems

Structuralism is my attempt to improve the technology of governing
